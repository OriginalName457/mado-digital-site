Throwing away a microwave, kettle, or other small appliance

Aug 10, 2026 · 4 min read

The four big appliances (fridge, washer, TV, air conditioner) have their own recycling law. Almost everything smaller, like a microwave, a kettle, a toaster, or a fan, follows easier rules. There are usually two good routes.

Small appliance recycling boxes

Japan runs a small home appliance recycling program. Many city offices, electronics stores, and supermarkets have a collection box or counter for things like kettles, hair dryers, game consoles, and cables. This is the greener route, since the metals get recovered.

Oversized or non-burnable garbage

If there is no collection box nearby, size decides the path. A small appliance under your ward's size limit often goes in non-burnable garbage. A larger one, like a microwave, is usually oversized garbage, booked and stickered like any sodai gomi item.

Anything with a battery

Cordless items with a built-in rechargeable battery need extra care, since those batteries are a fire risk. Look for an electronics store recycling box rather than the regular bin.

Phones, laptops, and tablets hold personal data. Wipe them before recycling, and use a maker take-back or a certified recycler rather than the household bin.
