Burnable or non-burnable? The items that trip everyone up

Jul 6, 2026 · 4 min read

Most of your daily trash splits into two buckets: burnable (moeru gomi, 燃えるごみ) and non-burnable (moenai gomi, 燃えないごみ). The names sound obvious until you are standing over two open bags with something that could go either way.

Burnable, even when it feels wrong

  • Used tissues, paper towels, and dirty paper
  • Most food scraps
  • Wooden chopsticks and toothpicks
  • Small garden leaves and clippings
  • In many wards, soft plastic with no recycling mark, and even diapers

Non-burnable, even when it feels burnable

  • Small metal items like scissors and cutlery
  • Broken ceramics and glass, wrapped in paper first so nobody gets cut
  • Light bulbs and small mirrors
  • Umbrellas and small appliances under the size limit

The honest catch

Wards disagree. Some burn almost everything because their incinerators are modern. Others sort strictly. A leather belt is burnable in one city and non-burnable in the next. Treat the lists above as the common pattern, then check your ward for the edge cases.

When something is genuinely both, like a plastic toy with metal parts, most wards say go by what it is mostly made of. If you cannot tell, ask.
